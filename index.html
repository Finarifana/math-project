GCD (PBB) Calculator



What is GCD?

Greatest Common Divisor (GCD) of two numbers is the largest integer that divides both numbers without leaving a remainder. GCD is often used in mathematics, algorithms, and computer programming.

Example: GCD of 12 and 18 is 6, because 6 is the largest integer that divides both numbers without leaving a remainder.

What is LCD?

Least Common Multiple (LCD) of two numbers is the smallest multiple that both numbers share. LCD is often used in mathematics, especially in solving problems involving fractions or ratios.

Example: LCD of 4 and 6 is 12, because 12 is the smallest multiple that both numbers share.
